The Workshop

Introducing the
GenAI Triage Workshop

The outcome: Your team walks away with a scored, prioritised GenAI roadmap your compliance team can sign off on.

The workshop applies the CX GenAI Prioritization Matrix — a two-axis decision framework — to your organisation's specific GenAI initiatives. Rather than debating ideas in the abstract, every initiative gets scored against three risk dimensions and plotted on the matrix. The result is a shared language for moving fast where it is safe, and stopping where it is not.

This is not a presentation or a training. It is a working session. Your team brings the initiatives; the framework provides the structure. By the end, every idea in the room has a quadrant, a posture, and a defensible business case.

The Prioritization Matrix

Every GenAI initiative maps to one of four quadrants based on CX impact potential and customer-facing risk. The scoring happens before the plotting — which is where most organisations skip a critical step.

🟢 Quick Wins

High impact, low risk. Build momentum and internal evidence here first.

🟡 Strategic Bets

High impact, high risk. Needs executive air cover, pilots, and defined guardrails.

⬜ Incremental Gains

Low impact, low risk. Good for ops efficiency. Don't oversell internally.

🔴 Danger Zone

Low impact, high risk. The hardest no to say, and the most important one.

Y-axis: CX Impact potential · X-axis: Audience Tolerance and Compliance Risk

The Risk Scorecard

Before any idea lands on the matrix, it passes through a three-factor scorecard: (1) the customer's emotional state at the point of AI contact, (2) your audience's AI tolerance — not assumed, but evidenced — and (3) your compliance burden in the relevant context. The scorecard surfaces assumptions your team didn't know they were making.
